Licensing roadmap — which exams do I need?

"Qualify as an asset manager" isn't one credential — it depends on your seat. Here's the full landscape.

PathExamsSponsor?Best for
Buy-side / RIASIE + Series 65No — take todayTrue AM, RIAs, fee-based advice, PM at advisory firms
Broker-dealerSIE + Series 7 + 63Yes (FINRA firm)Wealth mgmt, trading seat at BD, sell-side
Dual-registeredSIE + Series 7 + 66Yes (66 requires 7)Full flexibility: advise + trade + sell
Institutional PM / HFOften none; CFA matters—Systematic shops, hedge funds, institutional mandates
SpecializedSeries 3, 79, 24, 52Yes (varies)CTA/futures, IB, supervisor, muni bonds
Your practical playbook. Start with SIE (no sponsor needed). Then take Series 65 immediately after — also no sponsor needed, and it's the actual credential for buy-side PM work. That gets you a legitimate AM-relevant license in ~3 months without waiting for a firm. When you land a sponsor, add Series 7 + 63 (or 66) on top. This maximizes optionality.
